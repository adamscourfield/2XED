Important notes:
- `N3.4` and `N3.5` share the primary misconception `COLUMN_MULT_SHIFT_ERROR`: when multiplying by the tens digit, students write `122 × 2 = 244` rather than `122 × 20 = 2440`, omitting the zero placeholder. Both RT items use this exact error pattern drawn from the slide Skills Checks (Slide 46 for N3.4, Slide 48 for N3.5).
- `N3.5` ONB (28 × 3) is the simplest carrying case: one carry in the ones column.
- `N3.5` LRN-02 (47 × 23 = 1081, Slide 54 Skills Check) is the highest-load item: carrying required in both partial products.
- `N3.6` covers rectangles (Slide 56), triangles (Slide 63), and a real-world worded problem (William's paint, Slide 68). The RT reteach targets `AREA_HALF_MISSING` — students multiply base × height without halving for a triangle.

Primary misconceptions targeted:
- `N3.4`: `COLUMN_MULT_SHIFT_ERROR` — omits zero placeholder for tens partial product
- `N3.5`: `COLUMN_MULT_SHIFT_ERROR` (same error, now with carrying complicating the diagnosis)
- `N3.6`: `AREA_HALF_MISSING` — multiplies base × height for a triangle, forgetting to halve

Answer-mode intent in this pack:

- `N3.12`: NUMERIC throughout — LCM answers are plain numbers; LRN-02 is a real-world bus/train context
- `N3.13`: NUMERIC throughout — HCF answers are plain numbers; LRN-02 is a real-world PE teams context

Important notes:
- `N3.10` and `N3.11` are intentionally sequenced to expose the factor–multiple confusion before introducing LCM/HCF. The RT items directly address this reversal.
- `N3.10` RT targets `FACTOR_MULTIPLE_CONFUSION`: student says 7 is a multiple of 14 because 14 appears in the 7 times table (correct: 14 is a multiple of 7; 7 is a factor of 14).
- `N3.11` LRN-02 (factors of 16) uses tight distractors: 8 shares three of the four factors but has four factors total, while 64 has additional factors beyond 1, 2, 4, 8.
- `N3.12` ONB uses the coprime pair (3, 7) where LCM = product, to establish the concept before non-coprime pairs. LRN-01 (6, 9) and RT-01 (4, 6) both use non-coprime pairs to expose the `LCM_PRODUCT_ERROR`.
- `N3.13` LRN-02 (PE teams, HCF(70, 98) = 14) is the highest cognitive load item in this batch (level 3): students must recognise that "largest equal team size" maps to the HCF.

Primary misconceptions targeted:
- `N3.10`: `FACTOR_MULTIPLE_CONFUSION` — reverses the factor/multiple relationship direction
- `N3.11`: `FACTOR_DIRECTION_ERROR` — believes a larger number can be a factor of a smaller one
- `N3.12`: `LCM_PRODUCT_ERROR` — always multiplies the two numbers instead of finding the lowest common multiple
- `N3.13`: `FIRST_NOT_HIGHEST` — stops at the first common factor found rather than the highest

# Phase 1 Review Pack: N3.1 to N3.3

First offline-authored curriculum batch for N3.

File:
- `docs/unit-mapping/review-pack-phase1-n3-1-to-n3-3.jsonl`

Source:
- `(PART B) N1-3 Secondary Ready - Applications of Numeracy (FOUNDATION Not Meeting Expected Standard).pptx`
- Key slides: 4–5, 13, 15, 16, 28, 30–35

Scope:
- `N3.1` Properties of multiplication and division
- `N3.2` Mental strategies for multiplication and division
- `N3.3` Multiply and divide by powers of 10

Item count:
- `N3.1`: 4 items
- `N3.2`: 4 items
- `N3.3`: 4 items
- Total: `12`

Routing intent in `source.question_ref`:
- `ONB`: onboarding
- `LRN`: main learn/practice
- `RT`: reteach

Answer-mode intent in this pack:
- `N3.1`: SHORT for number family writing (multiple facts required); MCQ for concept/property checks
- `N3.2`: NUMERIC for direct recall and inverse-operation problems
- `N3.3`: NUMERIC throughout — answers are plain numbers, including the decimal reteach item

Important notes:
- `N3.1` uses bar-model-derived number families from Slides 5 and 13. The "number family" vocabulary matches the slide exactly. The RT item targets the specific slide example that division is not commutative (10 ÷ 2 ≠ 2 ÷ 10, Slide 28).
- `N3.2` uses the Skills Check examples from Slide 30 verbatim (8×9, 12×4, 56÷7, ?÷7=4).
- `N3.3` uses the exact worked examples from Slides 31–34: integer ×10/×100 (71×10, 821×100), the 'Related Calculations' pattern (given 3×4=12 find 30×4), and the decimal ×10 reteach (0.4×10). The RT item targets the `mult_m2_decimal_place` misconception confirmed in the findings doc: students append a zero (writing 0.40) rather than moving all digits one place left.
- All items are FOUNDATION tier (Part B source).

Primary misconceptions targeted:
- `N3.1`: `OVERGENERALISATION` — assuming division is commutative because multiplication is
- `N3.2`: `INVERSE_OPERATION_ERROR` — dividing or subtracting to find a missing dividend instead of multiplying
- `N3.3`: `mult_m2_decimal_place` — treating "multiply by 10" as "add a zero", which breaks for decimals

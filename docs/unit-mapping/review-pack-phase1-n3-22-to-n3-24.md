Important notes:
- `N3.22` LRN-02 (mean of 4 numbers = 6, add a fifth number, new mean = 5) requires working backwards: original total = 24, new total = 25, added number = 1. This is the highest cognitive load item in this batch.
- `N3.22` RT targets `MEAN_DIVISION_OMITTED`: student adds correctly (3 + 7 + 11 + 11 = 32) but forgets to divide by 4, writing 32 as the mean instead of 8.
- `N3.23` LRN-01 uses MCQ with distractors 36 (a square), 64 (a square), and 100 (a square) alongside 48 (not a square) to require genuine testing rather than guessing.
- `N3.23` RT targets `SQUARE_AS_MULTIPLY`: student writes 5² = 10 by computing 5 × 2 instead of 5 × 5 = 25.
- `N3.24` RT targets `ONE_IS_PRIME`: student claims 1 is prime because it has only one divisor. Correct: 1 is NOT prime (a prime must have exactly two distinct divisors: 1 and itself).
- `N3.24` LRN-02 (count of primes < 20) answer is 8: {2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19}.

Primary misconceptions targeted:
- `N3.22`: `MEAN_DIVISION_OMITTED` — sums the data values but forgets to divide by the count
- `N3.23`: `SQUARE_AS_MULTIPLY` — interprets the index as a multiplier (n² = n × 2) instead of an exponent (n × n)
- `N3.24`: `ONE_IS_PRIME` — incorrectly classifies 1 as a prime number
